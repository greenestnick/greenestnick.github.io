Master's Thesis
Neural Network based Digital Twin of a Half-Bridge LLC Resonant Converter

My Master's Thesis project used Neural Networks to predict the internal parameters of a power converter circuit. The goal is to reduce the sensing and modeling overhead needed to predict circuit parameters that are hard to measure directly include switch parasitics, and other circuit components as these can drift overtime due to degradation. After training the Neural Networks (one for each output), the range of the Mean Absolute Errors for all parameters was from 0.3% to 7%, with an average over all parameters of 2.3%.

Bayesian Image Deblurring

A custom Bayesian algorithm was used to deblur an image with a known Gaussian kernel form. By using Bayes' theorem and Metropolis-Hastings MCMC a non-blurred prediction is generated, which can then be blurred and compared with the input image. Over a series of iterations, the error is reduced until a final image is produced.

Raycaster Graphics Engine

This projects reimplements the 2.5 Graphics technique in games like Wolfenstein. These class of games us a 2D grid combined with raycasting to draw cubes where the wall height is related to the distance to the player. Interesting features of this game engine include textured walls and floor, different textures for each wall face, transparent sprites, directional sprites, transparent walls and doors, and the ability to look up and down.
